Now where to begin...?

As I already mentioned, I am currently serving as a Police Officer in the Metropolitan Police Service in London. I have been doing this for the past ten years and have picked up a wealth of experience in problem-solving, communications and risk management. I am now looking to start the next chapter of my life by transferring all of the skills I have learned so far and applying them to a career in technology.

I have been teaching myself the basics of Web Development over the past year and have utilised a number of free online resources. I started with Harvard's CS50 but, despite getting an insight into Computer Science and the foundations of programming, I found the course was beyond my skill level. I switched to the Responsive Web Design course provided by FreeCodeCamp.org and completed the sections on frontend development before overwhelming myself with the sections on backend development and databases.

Seeking a more visual and interactive teaching style, I switched to Udemy and enrolled on the 100 Days of Coding course by Acamind. I absolutely loved this course and felt that it really took my learning journey to the next level by getting my focused on creating projects, making mistakes and learning on the fly. After finishing this course, I started work on my first two projects: Trackman and Pints and Playgrounds. These were both fullstack projects that were great ways to put eveything I had learned on the course into practice.

I complimented this course with a Vue.js course, also by Acadamind, and started messing around with little frontend projects which were mostly by my daughter. These projects are by no means perfect or ground-breaking, but the important thing for me was that I got them to work as intended! I know that I will be able to tody the code up and polish them off as I gain more practice and experience.

Next Steps...

I am now working to improve my code readability and file structuring in preparation for working with others in a professional environment. I am also working on a good quality CV and this very website in order to best showcase my skills and abilities.

In the meantime, if you have any questions or just want to get in touch, please do not hesitate to contact me via any of the social links on this page!

Thanks again for reading!

-Ash
